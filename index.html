◆ Timeline
Thu Jun 18 · night before

Pack & prep

Car-pack both vehicles. Pre-make marinades & foil packets. Pre-crack eggs into a jar. Freeze water bottles (free ice + drinking water). Confirm reservation, check weather & fire ban. Charge every power bank.

Fri Jun 19 · 8–9 AM

Roll out of DDO

2-car convoy, lead car set. Gas up before the highway. Share the playlist across both cars.

Fri Jun 19 · ~10 AM

Road grocery stop

Last real store: IGA in Saint-Jérôme (Rivière-Rouge run) or Foodland in Gananoque (Ivy Lea run). Ice top-up, fresh produce, buns, breakfast sandwiches.

Fri Jun 19 · 12–1 PM

Arrive & set up

Both cars park. Pitch all tents first. Build the kitchen station. Buy firewood on-site. Light the first fire. Easy foil-packet dinner. Night-one campfire.

Sat Jun 20 · all day

The main day

Slow breakfast & coffee. Lake/river time — swim, canoe, kayak. No-cook lunch. Golden-hour hangs. Grill night. Long fire with games, music & s'mores.

Sun Jun 21 · AM

Wind down & pack out

Leftover brunch. Break down camp systematically across both cars. Leave-no-trace sweep. Check out (11 AM QC / 1 PM Ivy Lea). Hit "Reverse — route home" above for the drive back.

Safety

Print one copy per car

Campground phone (Plage 819-275-7757 / Ivy Lea 613-659-3057), nearest hospital & urgent care, and provincial fire/police non-emergency numbers. Don't rely on phones — service is spotty.

Water

Bring backup water

Both sites have potable tap water, but pack 10–15 L in jugs as backup. Never drink from the river or lake without a filter.

How to use WildWeekend

One shared trip, every device, live. Here's the 60-second version:

1

Add the crew — Basecamp tab → type each person's name. Names power everything else.

2

Pick the campsite — Campsites tab → vote with your name, then hit Choose this site on the winner. That unlocks maps & weather for it.

3

Claim your gear — Gear tab → assign one or more people per item (or "All"). Add or remove items; removed gear goes to a restorable archive.

4

Plan the drive — Itinerary tab → add stops (gas, grocery), then open the whole route in Google Maps. One tap reverses it for the drive home.

5

Shop smart — Shopping tab → food + gear checklists in 3 phases, with our best-store pick per gear item (Amazon / Canadian Tire / Decathlon).

6

Track the money — Costs tab → log expenses; the app computes who pays who to settle up.

7

Sign in (optional) — top right. Google sign-in identifies who's editing once Supabase is connected. The dot shows sync status: green = live, amber = this device only.

8

Take it offline — add it to your phone's home screen (Share → Add to Home Screen). The site keeps working without signal; it re-syncs when you're back online. You can also export the whole site as a PDF from Basecamp.
