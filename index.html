How to use Camping 2026

🔒 You're browsing signed out — the trip is read-only and crew pages are locked. Sign in (top right) to add the crew, claim gear, sync live, and unlock every feature.

One shared trip, every device, live. Here's the 60-second version:

1

Sign in to go live — top right. Signing in syncs the trip with the whole crew in real time and unlocks all themes. Signed out, you're in local (this-device) mode on the Classic theme.

2

Add the crew — Basecamp tab → type each person's name. Names power everything else.

3

Say who you are — Basecamp tab → pick yourself in the "I'm:" dropdown. This gives you a personal packing list and your own progress bar.

4

Pick the campsite — Campsites tab → vote with your name, then hit Choose this site. That switches the quick links, weather & route to that site, and shows its site map (zoom, pan, save).

5

Claim & pack your gear — Gear tab → assign people per item (or "All"). Your My packing list shows everything you need; tick items off as you pack, and add your own personal items.

6

Plan the drive — Itinerary tab → add stops, open the route in Google Maps, one tap reverses it for home.

7

Shop & split costs — Shopping tab for the 3-phase lists with store picks; Costs tab logs expenses and computes who pays who.

8

Make it yours — ⚙ Settings → switch themes, and turn on Simplify to hide extra tabs for a cleaner phone view. Add the site to your home screen (Share → Add to Home Screen) for an app icon + offline use.

📖 Admin guide

Private reference — only you (the owner) can see this. Everything here is invisible to other users.

🛠 What you can do as admin
  • Preview as any role — Live tab → see the site exactly as a signed-out visitor, visitor, camper, or leader sees it. A translucent amber bar appears at the very top; tap Exit preview to return.
  • Live presence — see who's online right now and which tab they're on.
  • Departure / announcement banner — Banner tab → compose a message (with optional link) shown to everyone at the top of Basecamp; toggle on/off anytime.
  • User management — Users tab → block/unblock emails, grant/revoke Leader, link or unlink any crew member to an email, and toggle whether new sign-ups are allowed.
  • Reports data center — live counters, visit analytics, time-on-site, and trip charts (see below).
  • Data tools — export/import the trip as JSON, export the whole site as a PDF, or reset all trip data.
🔑 How roles work
  • Signed-out — can only see Basecamp, Campsites, Itinerary, Activities & Survival. The Gear, Food, Shopping & Costs pages are hidden until they sign in.
  • Visitor — signed in but not linked to a crew member. Can view every page but cannot edit anything. Their email shows in your Users list and the Reports log.
  • Camper — signed in and linked to a crew member. Full edit access to crew, gear, food, costs, votes, etc. Can edit only their own packing list.
  • Leader — a camper you've promoted. Everything a camper can do, plus reset, edit costs/timeline, and manage any packing list. No admin panel. For live counts a leader is also counted as a camper, but keeps the Leader label.
  • Admin — you (). Everything, including this panel.
✉️ How sign-in & email-linking works
  • When a new email signs in for the first time, a centered prompt asks them to either pick an existing crew member, add themselves as new crew, or just browse.
  • Choosing/creating a crew member links their email to that name (stored privately — other users never see the email) and makes them a camper.
  • Choosing "just browsing" makes them a visitor — view-only. You'll still see their email in Users & Reports.
  • You can re-link or unlink any crew member from the Users tab. Unlinking frees the name so someone else can claim it.
  • The online counter tallies everyone currently on the site — signed-out, visitors, campers, leaders and admin combined.
📊 Reading the Reports data center
  • Live now — real-time counts by role, refreshed automatically from presence.
  • Audience — visits this month & all-time, unique people (by email vs anonymous), and total/average time on site.
  • Visitor log — every email that has visited, their role, visit count, time on site, and last-seen.
  • Analytics require the one-time analytics SQL to be run in Supabase; until then the panel shows a small "run the SQL" note.
